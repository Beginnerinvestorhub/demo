# Chapter 6: Banking Deep Dive

## 6.1 Banks vs. Credit Unions
While they offer similar services, their structures differ:
* **Banks**: For-profit, owned by shareholders. Usually offer better technology and more branches.
* **Credit Unions**: Not-for-profit cooperatives owned by members. Often offer lower loan rates and fewer fees.

## 6.2 FDIC and NCUA Insurance
Your money is protected up to $250,000 per depositor, per institution.
* **FDIC**: Protects bank deposits.
* **NCUA**: Protects credit union deposits.



## 6.3 Understanding Overdrafts
An overdraft occurs when you spend more than is in your account. While "Overdraft Protection" sounds helpful, it often carries high fees (avg. $35). Many modern banks now offer fee-free "buffer" zones.
