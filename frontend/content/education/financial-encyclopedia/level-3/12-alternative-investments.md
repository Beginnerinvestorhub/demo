# Chapter 12: Alternative Investments

## 12.1 Beyond Stocks and Bonds
Alternative investments are assets that don't fit into traditional categories. They often have low correlation with the stock market, providing a hedge during downturns.

## 12.2 Common Alternatives
* **Commodities**: Physical goods like gold, oil, or agricultural products.
* **Private Equity**: Investing in companies that are not publicly traded.
* **Hedge Funds**: Pooled funds that use non-traditional strategies (shorting, leverage) to earn active returns.



## 12.3 Portfolio Construction
The goal of Chapter 12 is to move from "buying assets" to "building a portfolio." This involves:
1. **Asset Allocation**: Deciding the % of Stocks vs. Bonds vs. Alts.
2. **Correlation**: Ensuring your assets don't all move in the same direction at the same time.
